Aerial Infrastructure

The Skyway

A structured aerial routing network enabling safe, scalable, and autonomous high-speed flight outside traditional airport infrastructure.

Free-flight autonomy does not scale. Without structure, noise, safety, and congestion compound rapidly, certification becomes impossible, and operations remain tied to centralized pads and short hops. The Skyway defines explicit routes, entry and exit points, altitude layers, and operational rules that allow vehicles with different capabilities to coexist and operate predictably.

Development Sequence

01

Airspace Definition & Operational Framework

Define current routes and new route operations including entries, exits, merges, altitude overlays, operational terms, and vehicular capability requirements within this airspace. Establish network necessities and foundational requirements for the Skyway infrastructure.

02

Network Generation Algorithm

Research and develop the algorithm to programmatically generate the Skyway network. This includes defining entries, exits, merges, splits, and route structures across multiple altitude layers while accounting for geographic and regulatory constraints.

03

Constraint-Aware Pathfinding

Develop a constraint-aware routing system for optimal path calculation between any two points using the Skyway for traversal. The algorithm considers vehicle performance envelope, energy state, noise restrictions, traffic density, and real-time conditions.

Vehicle Integration

Skyway accessibility is determined by vehicle capability, not operator preference. Mapped routes and their accessibility are directly tied to verified vehicle performance envelopes. Two vehicles may take different paths between the same points based on their individual capabilities and constraints.

Policy & Regulatory Path

Skyway deployment requires regulatory evolution, not regulatory avoidance. Policy considerations are integrated from day one to ensure compatibility with certification standards, safety requirements, and public acceptance frameworks.
